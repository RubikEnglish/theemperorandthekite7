"The Emperor and the Kite"

"El Emperador y la Cometa"




61. Then he leaned farther out of the tower window and grasped the heavy strand.



62. He brought it into his tower room and loosened the string of the kite.



63. He set the kite free, saying, “Go to thy home in the sky, great kite.” And the kite flew off toward the heavens.



64. Then the emperor tied one end of the thick strand to the heavy iron bar across the window, and the other end stretched all the way down to Djeow Seow’s tiny hands.



65. The emperor stepped to the window sill, slipped under the iron bar, and slid down the rope.



66. His robes billowed out around him like the wings of a bright kite.



67. When his feet reached the ground, he knelt before his daughter. And he touched the ground before her with his lips.



68. Then he rose and embraced her, and she almost disappeared in his arms.



69. With his arm encircling her, the emperor said, “Come to thy home with me, loyal child.”



70. He lifted the tiny princess to his shoulders and carried her all the way back to the palace.
